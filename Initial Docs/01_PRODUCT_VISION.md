# Pulse Product Vision

## Product statement

Pulse is a self-hosted, Docker-first Music Workspace that helps DJs understand, organize, prepare, review, and move music across professional DJ ecosystems without surrendering control of their files or metadata.

Pulse is not merely a dashboard, tag editor, player, playlist manager, or clone of Lexicon, Mixed in Key, or One Tagger. It combines the strongest ideas of those categories in a coherent workspace while keeping suggestions reversible and the user authoritative.

## Core promise

One place to:

- See and search every track.
- Understand audio, metadata, quality, structure, and confidence.
- Organize music into flexible crates without duplicating files.
- Prepare cues, energy, moods, tags, and sets.
- Review uncertainty and resolve problems intentionally.
- Sync or export safely to Traktor, Rekordbox, Serato, VirtualDJ, and CDJ workflows.
- Preserve control across NAS, local disks, external drives, and future companion devices.

## Primary user

A working DJ with a growing multi-location library who uses a Mac for performance and preparation, stores a larger catalog on NAS, and needs accurate organization without subscriptions or opaque automatic changes.

## Product principles

1. **User authority:** AI suggests; the user approves. No destructive silent overwrite.
2. **Local truth:** files, availability, and device reality are represented honestly.
3. **Everything searchable:** search is a first-class action, not a distant screen.
4. **Action over navigation:** common work should take few steps.
5. **Reversible change:** metadata, rules, duplicate decisions, and sync operations have preview and recovery paths.
6. **Progressive depth:** calm overview first; professional detail on demand.
7. **Native feeling:** Apple-inspired clarity, motion, materials, keyboard behavior, and hierarchy.
8. **Workspace memory:** views, filters, sizing, and user choices persist appropriately.
9. **Separation of concerns:** web/backend services and the macOS desktop companion have different responsibilities.
10. **Honest status:** pending, unavailable, local-only, uncertain, and failed are distinct states.

## Success outcomes

- Find any known track or category in seconds.
- Reduce manual metadata and preparation work while increasing confidence.
- Prepare a track from discovery to export without switching among many utilities.
- Avoid duplicate audio files while supporting multiple organizational contexts.
- Know what changed, why, and whether it has synced.
- Operate when the backend or NAS is temporarily unavailable where technically possible.

## Non-goals for the initial product

- Streaming-service replacement.
- DAW or full music-production suite.
- Social network or public marketplace.
- Cloud-first SaaS dependency.
- Fully autonomous AI that modifies the library without review.
- Mobile performance client.
- Premature parity with every DJ application.

## Experience sentence

Pulse should feel like a beautifully designed native music operating environment: quiet when the library is healthy, powerful when work is required, and transparent whenever it proposes a change.
